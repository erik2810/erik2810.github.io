My research develops at the interface of mathematical physics and geometric machine learning. Starting from a foundation in variational principles, symmetry, and field theory, I build differentiable computational frameworks that model how structured geometry emerges from local interactions — energy-based modeling of geometric graphs where discrete topology and continuous 3D geometry are inseparably coupled.

01Fields of Work

01 / RESEARCH

Research Vision

I study how complex geometric structure emerges from simple local constraints. My approach integrates variational principles and energy-based formulations from mathematical physics with modern graph neural network architectures, aiming to learn generative models for structured systems — branching morphologies, meshes, and embedded graphs in 3D space.

Read more →
02 / SIMULATION

Differentiable Physics

I develop mesh-based differentiable physics simulators entirely in PyTorch, where mesh topology initializes particle–spring systems and physical forces arise from energy-based formulations. These systems are fully differentiable, enabling gradient-based optimization through complex physical dynamics.

View project →
03 / LEARNING

Graph Machine Learning

Graphs embedded in 3D space are neither purely geometric nor purely combinatorial. My research direction focuses on generative models for such hybrid structures, combining topological reasoning, geometric invariance, and structural priors within discrete–continuous learning frameworks.
